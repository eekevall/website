UK Household Food Shopping

Advanced analytical analysis and dashboard design - a machine learning analysis using Python and Tableau to analyse income and spending on food data.

Poor diet within the UK is a public health issue and the UK is ranked as one of the worst in Europe for levels of obesity. 'Ultra-processed' food accounted for more than half of all family food purchases in the UK, this is higher than other European countries. What types of food are UK households buying? Do they differ and have there been changes over the years?

The analysis in Python (libraries: pandas | numpy | os | pyreadstat | gc | matplotlib pyplot | seaborn | scipy | folium | json | sklearn | statsmodels api) invloved machine learning regression, clustering and times series analysis and resulted in an interactive Tableau Dashboard.

Planning for Influenza Season

Predictive analysis using Microsoft Excel to help plan for Influenza Season in the United States.

During the United States' influenza season some people, particularly those in vulnerable populations, end up in the hospital and additional staff are needed. A staffing agency needs to determine when to send staff, and how many, to each state. Hypothesis: If states have a high proportion of vaccinated health care workers (nurses, physician assistants and doctors), then fewer vulnerable people will develop serious complications and end up in the hospital.

The data mapping and integration, statistical analysis and hypothesis testing were carried out in Excel, visualised in Tableau and presented in a video.

Imperial Business Analytics Capstone Project

A finance optimisation problem to investigate the feasibility of a car manufacturing company producing mechanical ventilators while maximising profits.

Health authorities around the world are doing their utmost to procure more ventilators for medical facilities treating COVID-19 patients. The government has asked if the car manufacturing company can help with the shortages. Producing ventilators during the pandemic is obviously for the public good, and would benefit thier image, but is it feasible?

The linear optimisation problem was solved using AMPL and Google ORTools and the results provided in a presentation.
